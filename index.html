📉 Diurnal Temp Range
What is Diurnal Temperature Range?

Diurnal Temperature Range (DTR) is the difference between the highest (day) and lowest (night) temperature in a single day.

E.g. if day is 34°C and night is 24°C, DTR = 10°C.

A larger DTR means hotter days and cooler nights. A smaller DTR feels muggy and uncomfortable all day.

💡 DTR is important for agriculture — crops need cool nights to recover from daytime heat.

😓 Comfort Index
What is the Comfort Index?

The Comfort (Discomfort) Index measures how comfortable the weather feels based on temperature and humidity.

Below 21: Comfortable
21–24: Mild discomfort
24–27: Uncomfortable — most feel hot
Above 27: Severe — health risk

💡 Higher values = hotter and more humid conditions.
